Benchmarking results summary🔗

These plots were generated on Saturday, July 18th, 2026, from commit bce8808

Benchmarks are organized into groups, one per subdirectory of a run’s data folder. Each group gets its own section below, and within a section every benchmark that group produced is shown. The plots are generated from the data in the data/ directory.

Each ordinary benchmark is shown with three plots side-by-side — timing (real, cpu and gc time), number of recursive calls, and total size — each tracked across benchmark runs.

Scaling benchmarks (those produced by the scale form) are rendered differently: their metrics are reported across a series of expressions from the most recent run rather than across runs. They appear inline within whichever group they belong to, mixed among the ordinary benchmarks, and are flagged with a (scaling benchmark) tag next to their name.

Maximum-argument benchmarks (those produced by the max-arg form) are also rendered specially: a single plot tracking the largest feasible argument across runs. They too appear inline within their group, flagged with a (max-arg benchmark) tag.

Additionally, there are benchmarks for the code profiler in the Extra plots section. These are generated from the files in the extra directory and are not part of the normal benchmarking data.

You can find these files and data in the roulette-bench-data repository.

These benchmarks are re-generated with each commit to the roulette repository.
